About
A bit about me
The short version — and where I've been so far

I'm a physicist who got hooked on photonics — specifically on what happens when you push optical measurements to their quantum limits. My journey started with theoretical quantum noise at MSU, then took me through precision optomechanics at EPFL, and now to building photonic neural networks at KAUST.

I care about understanding things deeply before building them. Most of my work sits at the boundary between theory and experiment — deriving what should happen, then figuring out how to make it happen on a chip.

Outside the lab, I read a lot, try to stay active, and occasionally write about things I find interesting. I believe good science comes from curious people who have lives beyond their papers.
